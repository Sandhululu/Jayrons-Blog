Got this book recommendation from Emma Likeman. I should have defo read it earlier than I actually did but oh well. Also this is Elly's favourite book so I wanted to read it and talk to her about it before I see her for 4AM Kru

You can definitely tell that this is a Murakami book there is something about his prose that is very unique. The main similarity this has to Kafka On the Shore is the philosophical undertones present during the book. This was a very heavy and a sad book to the say the least. It definitely impacted me more than those emotions to I used previously but i'm not too sure how to describe my emotions in words.

This book had me just as confused as Watanabe for most of it. It perfectly encapsulates the chaos and the wierdness of love in your early 20s. The main outcome I took from the Naoko story is that no matter how much you want to help someone it's ultimately up to them to try there best and sometimes the best just isn't enough. However attempts should still be tried no matter what I believe that Watanabe really did love Naoko and tried his best but he lost the ability to look after himself and became very reliant on Midori. It's wierd that even before Naoko died it still seemed that Naoko herself was dead yet Watanabe stayed hooked as long as he could. I find this very honourable but also it distracts him from the pain he instills on Midori and how obviously Midori wants to be with him. In a fucked up way Naoko rubs off so much on Watanabe that he ends up just like and the roles are completely reversed with Midori desperate to have Watanabe open up to her and Watanabe shutting off just like Naoko closes herself from Watanabe and cant stopm that.

This book had me feeling strange nostalgia towards my own past relationship even though that was so long ago. I think thats where some of the personal beauty comes from despite obviously not having the same experience as Watanabe some of his problems deeply resonated with me. Reiko's ending conversation to Watanabe is what I will take away from this book. Always remember sweet past memories but don't let the rush of nostalgia blind you from what you currently have in the present.
