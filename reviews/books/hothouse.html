I was interested in reading this because it's made by Jason's grandad Brian and also because it's quite an early work in science fiction, so I was hoping for a fresh perspective on Science Fiction. This book definitely gave me a fresh perspective. The setting is definitely the best part of this book. It is set some point long into the future after a cosmic event that caused the sun to be in sync with the moon. This caused half the world to be in eternal sunshine and the other half to be eternally dark. Due to the eternal sunshine, hothouse conditions were formed and the plant world took over the human world.

The most fascinating bit of this book is seeing mankind no longer be the apex predator. Another cool plant is the traverser which is like a huge spider that can travel between the Earth and the Moon. However, due to the setting-based story front, I felt that character development was left on the side. There are some interesting interactions between the Morel and Gren and how this affected his relationship with Poyly and then Yattmur. But other than that, the whole Lily-yo starting and them becoming flymen and their quest is completely ignored throughout the book.

To conclude this was a very creative book which has a very rich setting full of lots of imagination but the plot and character writing left much to be desired.
